What Are Options?

Learn the fundamental concept of options contracts and understand how they differ from traditional stock ownership.

By the end of this lesson, you will:

  • ‚úÖ Understand what an options contract is
  • ‚úÖ Know the difference between rights and obligations
  • ‚úÖ Identify underlying assets and contract specifications
  • ‚úÖ Recognize how options differ from stocks

1 Introduction to Options

Options are financial derivatives that give the buyer the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a predetermined price within a specific time period.

üîë Key Concept

Think of options like a "reservation" at a restaurant. You pay a small fee (premium) to reserve the right to buy a meal (underlying asset) at a fixed price (strike price) within a certain time (expiration). You can choose to exercise your reservation or let it expire.

‚ö° Quick Check: Options vs. Stocks

üìà Stock Ownership

  • Ownership: You own the actual stock
  • Risk: Unlimited (stock can go to $0)
  • Cost: Full price of shares
  • Time: No expiration

üé´ Options Contract

  • Ownership: Right to buy/sell, not ownership
  • Risk: Limited to premium paid
  • Cost: Small premium (vs. full stock price)
  • Time: Expires on specific date

2 Options Contract Specifications

Every options contract has specific characteristics that define its terms and conditions:

üéØ Underlying Asset

The security that the option is based on (stock, ETF, index, etc.)

Example: Apple Inc. (AAPL) stock

üí∞ Strike Price

The price at which you can buy or sell the underlying asset

Example: $150 strike price on AAPL

üìÖ Expiration Date

The date when the option contract expires

Example: March 15, 2025

üíµ Premium

The price you pay to purchase the options contract

Example: $2.50 per share

3 Rights vs. Obligations

Understanding the difference between rights and obligations is crucial to grasping how options work.

‚úÖ Rights (What You Can Do)

  • ‚Ä¢
    Choose to exercise: Buy or sell at strike price
  • ‚Ä¢
    Let it expire: Walk away, losing only the premium
  • ‚Ä¢
    Sell the option: Before expiration to capture remaining value

‚ùå Obligations (What You Must Do)

  • ‚Ä¢
    Pay the premium: Upfront cost when buying
  • ‚Ä¢
    Nothing else: No further financial obligation
  • ‚Ä¢
    Market timing: Must exercise by expiration

üí° Important Distinction

With options, you have maximum flexibility but limited risk. This asymmetry is what makes options so powerful for traders.

5 What Happens When Options Expire?

Understanding expiration is crucial because it affects how you manage your options positions.

‚è∞ Expiration Scenarios

üìà In-The-Money (ITM)

When the option has intrinsic value

Call ITM: Stock price > Strike price
Put ITM: Stock price < Strike price
Result: May be automatically exercised

üìâ Out-of-The-Money (OTM)

When the option has no intrinsic value

Call OTM: Stock price < Strike price
Put OTM: Stock price > Strike price
Result: Expires worthless

üéØ At-The-Money (ATM)

When stock price equals strike price

Call ATM: Stock price = Strike price
Put ATM: Stock price = Strike price
Result: Expires worthless (no intrinsic value)

üí° Important: Automatic Exercise

Most brokers automatically exercise ITM options at expiration. However, it's generally better to close positions before expiration to avoid unexpected outcomes and commissions.

üíº Real-World Example: Earnings Play

Scenario: Apple Earnings Bet

You're bullish on Apple (AAPL) stock before their earnings report. Current stock price: $150

üìà Stock Investment
  • Buy 100 shares @ $150 = $15,000
  • If AAPL ‚Üí $160: +$1,000 profit
  • If AAPL ‚Üí $140: -$1,000 loss
  • Return: ¬±6.7%
üé´ Options Strategy
  • Buy 1 call @ $2.50 = $250
  • If AAPL ‚Üí $160: +$950 profit
  • If AAPL ‚Üí $140: -$250 loss
  • Return: +380% or -100%
Key Insights:
  • ‚Ä¢ Leverage: Options provide 10x leverage in this example
  • ‚Ä¢ Risk Control: Maximum loss is limited to premium paid
  • ‚Ä¢ Capital Efficiency: Same market exposure with 1/60th capital
  • ‚Ä¢ Flexibility: Can walk away if wrong, keeping remaining capital

7 Key Terms Summary

Before taking the quiz, let's review the most important terms you'll need to know:

üìã Options Contract

üìà Call Option

üìâ Put Option

The right to sell the underlying asset at the strike price.

üéØ Strike Price

The predetermined price at which you can buy or sell the underlying asset.

üí∞ Premium

The price you pay to purchase the options contract.

üìÖ Expiration Date

The date when the options contract expires.

üèÜ In-The-Money (ITM)

‚ùå Out-of-The-Money (OTM)
